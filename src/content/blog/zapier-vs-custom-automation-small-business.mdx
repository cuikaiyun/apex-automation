# Zapier vs. Custom Automation: Which One Is Right for Your Small Business?

If you've looked into automating business workflows, you've probably heard of Zapier. It's a powerful tool — and for certain use cases, it's exactly the right choice. But it's not the right choice for everything, and understanding the difference can save you time, money, and frustration.

Here's a plain-language breakdown of when to use Zapier (or Make, its main competitor) and when a custom solution makes more sense.

---

## What Zapier Is Good At

Zapier excels at connecting two or more existing software tools when those tools are already in Zapier's library (which is extensive — 5,000+ apps). Classic use cases:

- New form submission → create a contact in your CRM + send a Slack notification
- New invoice in QuickBooks → log to a Google Sheet + notify your accountant
- New e-commerce order → add to fulfillment queue + send customer confirmation

These are linear, trigger-action workflows. If your process is "when X happens, do Y and Z," Zapier is often the fastest and most cost-effective solution.

---

## Where Zapier Falls Short

Zapier starts to struggle when your workflow has conditional logic, requires real-time processing at scale, involves data that needs transformation or enrichment, or needs to interact with tools that aren't in Zapier's library.

> 💡 If you find yourself stacking 10+ steps in a Zap with multiple filters and formatters, you've likely outgrown what Zapier does elegantly.

Common signs you've hit the ceiling:

- Zaps break frequently when upstream tools update their APIs
- Your automation costs more per month than expected because of high task volume
- You need to maintain data state across multiple steps or sessions
- Your process requires human-in-the-loop approval at certain stages

---

## When Custom Automation Makes More Sense

Custom automation — whether that's a purpose-built script, API integration, or internal application — is the right call when:

- You need logic that no-code tools can't express cleanly
- You're processing high volumes where per-task pricing becomes expensive
- You need a user interface (not just background automation)
- Your data is sensitive and you want control over where it flows
- You're automating something core to your business that needs to be reliable and maintainable

---

## The Hybrid Approach

The most pragmatic answer for most small businesses is a hybrid: use Zapier for simple integrations that can be set up quickly, and invest in custom solutions for your core workflows. You don't need to pick one or the other.
